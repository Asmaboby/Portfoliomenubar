Background

The Grameen Bikash Foundation (GBF) is a Non-Government,Non-political, Non-profitable and Voluntary Organization.Grameen Bikash Foundation (GBF) has been working in Bangladesh since the beginning of its inception in 2003.

The GBF is a National Non-Government Organization (NGO) with registration authority:Directorate of Social Welfare No: Bogra 1094,Dated: 16/08/2004 and NGO Affairs Bureau, No: 2521, dated:03/12/2009.It is guided by a constitution for management and control of its affairs.

In foundation nature, our purpose is to provide services of international standard ensuring the most participative and cost-effective way out to our valued partners with an aim to render high excellence and demand driven services in terms of consulting service,advice,guidance and assixtance by understanding the partners's needs.

Vision

We seek a Bangladesh of hopes;less poverty where people will live in dignity with social security

Mission

To reduce poverty by giving need based assistance and proper guidence to the community people.

Monitoring and Evaluation:

Grameen Bikash Foundation (GBF) with a multi-disciplinary team of experts and professionals has been successfully managing and implementing a series of consultancy assignments of survey,research, monitoring & evauation,data management & ICT projects since its commencement in 2003.Specialized in quantitative and conventional methods ,GBF also carries out its assignments following non-conventional qualitative tools and methods of Participatory Rural Apprasial (PRA), Focus Group Discussion (FGD), Key Informations Intervies (KII), Case study etc, and whenever appropriate usues combined methods too.GBF provides monitoring and evaluation services including verificattion of program outputs (i.e. tangible products and services),both in physical and financial terms for improved and effective management and implementation of the projects. Besides, GBF provides technical and logistics supports through conducting training on research methodology, questionnaire development and data management, socio-econ omic impact assessment,among others,related to organizational capacity development.GBF has been working with Chaars Livelihood Programme (CLP) (www.clp-bangladesh.org) in collecting Asset Transfer Programme monthly income & expenditure monitoring data of phase 1 and phase 2 participants in Jamalpur, Sirajganj,Bogra,Gaibandha,Kurigram ,Rangpur, Lalmonirhat and Nilphamary district in sample basis as a monitoring partner since March 2008 to March 2016.More than eighty CLP assignments have been completed successfully that include tasks such as baseline of homestead gardening,HH consumption, Market development Baseline Survey,Cattle Birth & Mortality, Milk Production and Cattle maintenance study,Relative Profitability of Crossbred versus Local cattle,demographic,third party verification,external monitoring,socio-economic analysis,agricul tral, Poverty Assessment and livelihoods related studies,Nutrition status study, data managemnet and analysis.

GBF has gained extensive experience on FtF indicators by working with USAID Horticulture Project and World Vegetable Center (AVRDC),World vision Bangladesh NJ project and CARE-Bangladesh.GBF has long lerm subcontract with AVRDC and Swisscontract-M4C.GBF has proven experience to conducted number of large scale baseline surveys.Major clients are Chars Livelihoods Programme (CLP), iDE, Swisscontract, AVRDC and International Potato Center (CIP), CARE-Bangladesh, World Vision Bangladesh NJ project.

Sustainable Primary Education Project (SPEP):

GBF is operated Sustainable Primary Educaion Project (SPEP),it is an innovation grant under the Directorate of Primary Education.This pre-schooling grant is for one year.GBF operated 8 pre-schooling center in 2 unions of Dhunut Upazila under Bogra district.This pre schooling activities for the children was a good initiative taken by the Government.This initiative is very much helpful for the rural children of remote areas,and in this regards GBF is continuing the same activity with its own fund in 2 centers in Dhunut Uapazilla under Bogra district.

Old Home Initiatives:
